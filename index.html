The plan


The Winged Wanderers are organizing a tour through the western ghats. A superb chance for ornithologists around the world, it is a very informative trail. You wil get a chance to find some of the endangered species hidden in the wilderness of the western ghats. Besides providing the lust of searching for the hidden, this trail also provides the placidity of the nature. A must for all bird lovers...

The main Atrractions
The pale-billed flowerpecker or Tickell's flowerpecker is a tiny bird that feeds on nectar and berries, found in India, Bangladesh and Sri Lanka. The bird is common especially in urban gardens with berry bearing trees.
The black-naped monarch or black-naped blue flycatcher is a slim and agile passerine bird belonging to the family of monarch flycatchers found in Southeast Asia
The Rufous Babbler is seen in small noisy flocks. They are often seen foraging for insects on the forest floor amidst leaf litter. Often, it is their loud nasal call that gives their presence away.
One would see small flocks of Blue-winged Parakeets dashing through the forests. Their long blue tails, tipped yellow give away their identity. These parakeets are fairly common across the Western Ghats. They also occur in some parts of the Eastern Ghats.
The primary diet of Malabar Grey Hornbill birds is fruits. Their loud cackling call is also very characteristic. Their nesting behaviour is similar to the other hornbills. These too nest in tree hollows.
